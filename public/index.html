Balasana (Child's Pose)
Beginner
Calms the nervous system and helps relieve stress, anxiety, and mental fatigue.
1 Kneel and sit on your heels.
2 Exhale and lay your torso down between your thighs.

Viparita Karani (Legs Up)
Beginner
Eases muscle cramps, restless legs, and promotes deep relaxation for better sleep.
1 Lie on your back near a wall.
2 Swing your legs up against the wall.
3 Keep your arms relaxed at your sides and hold for 5 mins.

Supta Baddha Konasana
Intermediate
Reduces physical tension in the core and chest, regulating panic and heart rate.
1 Lie flat on your back.
2 Bring the soles of your feet together, knees falling outward.
3 Rest one hand on your heart and one on your belly.

Professional Counselling & Therapy
Physical detox is only the first step. Emotional and mental support is crucial for long-term recovery.

Understanding the Psychological Impact of Withdrawal

Withdrawal is not just a physical challenge; it is profoundly emotional. As your body adjusts to the absence of a substance, your brain chemistry undergoes significant changes. This often leads to severe anxiety, depression, mood swings, and intense cravings. Understanding that these feelings are a normal part of the healing process is the first step toward overcoming them.

Our anonymous counselling services are designed to provide you with a safe, judgment-free space to explore these emotions. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) are proven techniques used by our specialists to help you identify triggers, manage stress, and build resilient coping mechanisms.

How Our Anonymous Therapy Works

  • 100% Confidentiality: Your identity is never revealed. You communicate using your HLO-ID.
  • Asynchronous Messaging: Reach out whenever a craving or panic attack hits. Doctors review and reply comprehensively.
  • Holistic Approach: Our psychologists work in tandem with medical doctors to ensure both your mind and body are treated safely.
In Crisis?

If you are experiencing suicidal thoughts or a severe mental health crisis, please use the Emergency Alert feature in your patient dashboard immediately after logging in.
